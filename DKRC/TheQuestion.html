Why is Surface Area Important to Normal Cell Function?

  • "A high surface-to-volume ratio facilitates the exchange of materials between a cell and its environment."
  • Implicit commonsense and biological knowledge
  • The answer is in the explanation - the reasoning
    • Assurance is not enough

Implicit Commonsense Knowledge

  • Every cell has a surface area and a surface-to-volume ratio
  • Surface area is proportional to surface-to-volume ratio
  • Proportional is symmetric and transitive
  • If something is important, and is proportional to another thing, then the other thing is also important
    (importance is transferred by proportionality)

Implicit Biological Knowledge

  • Every cell has an environment.
  • Every cell and environment have an exchange rate
  • The exchange rate is important.

Two Encodings for ATP

  • Shallow, which gives the intuition
  • Deep, which gives the power
  • Shallow ≠ Deep
