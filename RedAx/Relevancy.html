Relevancy Measures

Aboutness

  • Is an axiom "about" the same things as the conjecture?
  • Fraction of the axiom's symbols that occur in the conjecture
  • Example
    • Conjecture: ![X]:( even(X) => ?[Y]:equal(Y,divide(X,2)) )
    • Axiom: ~?[X]:( greater(X,2) & even(X) & prime(X) )
    • 2 of the 4 axiom symbols occur in the conjecture: even, 2
      Axiom score = 2/4 = 0.5

Linkages

  • Measures global linkages between clauses
  • Consider each unique symbol/arity pair SA
    • A term SA relevance is ( # of unique SAs / # of SA occurences )
    • A predicate SA relevance is ( number of literals in set / number of ~SA literals )
    • Literal relevance is the product of its literal and predicate relevances
    • Clause relevance is the sum of its literal relevances

Hierarchies

  • Theories are often naturally layered, through use of definitions
  • Axiom relevance measured by distance from layer(s) of conjecture
