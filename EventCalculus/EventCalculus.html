Event Calculus

Structure

  • A formalism for reasoning about actions over time
  • Time is continuous
  • Fluents take on different values in different situations
  • Events initiate and terminate changes to fluents
    Events release fluents from the commonsense law of inertia
  • Discrete event calculus limits time to integer time points

Language

  • happens(E,T): Event E occurs at timepoint T.
  • holdsAt(F,T): Fluent F is true at T.
  • releasedAt(F,T): F is released from the commonsense law of inertia at T.
  • initiates(E,F,T): If E occurs at T, then F is true and not released at T+1.
  • terminates(E,F,T): If E occurs at T, then F is false and not released at T+1.
  • releases(E,F,T): If E occurs at T, then F is released at T+1.
  • trajectory(F1,T1,F2,T2): If F1 is initiated at T1, and T2 > 0, then F2 is true at T1+T2.
  • antiTrajectory(F1,T1,F2,T2): If F1 is terminated at T1, and T2 > 0, then F2 is true at T1+T2.

Examples

  • Axiom DEC1 
    ∀T1,F,T2 (stoppedIn(T1,F,T2) 
    ⇔ 
∃E,T (T1 < T < T2 | happens(E,T) | terminates(E,F,T) ))

  • ∀T,H1,H2 ((holdsAt(waterLevel(H1),T) & holdsAt(waterLevel(H2),T)) ⇒ H1 = H2)
